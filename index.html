Air Pollution 101

What is Air Pollution?
Air pollution occurs when harmful substances, like gases and particulates, accumulate in the atmosphere. These pollutants come from both natural and human activities and affect both the environment and health.

Common Sources of Pollution

  • Industrial Emissions: Factories release pollutants like sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides.
  • Vehicle Emissions: Cars and buses emit harmful gases, especially in cities.
  • Agriculture: Fertilizers and livestock produce methane and other pollutants.
  • Natural Events: Wildfires, volcanic eruptions, and dust storms can introduce pollutants into the air.

Health Impacts of Air Pollution

  • Respiratory Issues: Pollutants like PM10 can lead to asthma and other breathing problems.
  • Heart Conditions: Long-term exposure can increase risks of cardiovascular diseases.
  • General Health Effects: Air pollution can also impact the immune system and lead to other health issues.

Global Efforts and How You Can Help

Governments and organizations are working to reduce emissions through policies and renewable energy. You can help by using public transport, conserving energy, and supporting green initiatives.
